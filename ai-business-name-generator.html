Why Your Business Name Matters More Than You Think

Your business name is the first impression you make on every potential customer. It appears on your Google listing, your storefront, your business cards, your invoices, and every email you send. A strong name builds trust before anyone even interacts with you. A weak name creates friction.

Research from the Kellogg School of Management found that companies with easier-to-pronounce names see higher stock performance in their first year of trading. The same principle applies to small businesses: if people can't say your name, they can't recommend you to friends.

7 Tips for Choosing the Right Business Name

Generating names is the fun part. Choosing the right one takes a bit more thought. Here's what actually matters.

1

Say it out loud 10 times

If it's awkward to say, it's awkward to recommend. Your name needs to survive the "tell a friend at a noisy dinner" test.

2

Check the domain before you fall in love

A great name with no domain available is a dead end. Look for .com first, then .co, .io, or industry-specific extensions.

3

Search the USPTO trademark database

Before printing business cards, search tmsearch.uspto.gov to make sure nobody owns the name in your industry.

4

Avoid names that box you in

"Austin Cupcakes" sounds great until you move to Dallas or start selling cookies. Leave room to grow.

5

Check social media handles

Consistent branding across Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, and X matters. Search for handle availability early.

6

Google the name in quotes

Search "your name" in quotes. If there's an existing business, a slang term, or something embarrassing on page 1 — keep looking.

7

Sleep on it for 48 hours

Excitement fades. If you still love the name after two days and it passes all the checks above, you've found it.

What makes a good business name?
A strong business name is easy to spell, easy to pronounce, and memorable after hearing it once. It should work as a domain name, look good in a logo, and not have unintended meanings in other languages if you plan to go international. The best names evoke a feeling or hint at what you do without being too literal.
